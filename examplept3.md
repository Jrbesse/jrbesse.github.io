## Overview

John 13-21 shifts from public ministry to Jesus' final hours with his closest
followers, then to his death and resurrection. The tone turns intimate -
long private conversations replace public debates.

## Key Passages

### John 13-17 - The Upper Room Discourse

Jesus washes the disciples' feet, predicts his betrayal, and gives an
extended farewell - covering love, the Holy Spirit, prayer, and what it means
to remain connected to him ("I am the vine").

### John 18-19 - Trial and Crucifixion

Jesus is arrested, tried, and crucified. John highlights Jesus' composure and
control throughout - this isn't something happening *to* him so much as
something he's walking into.

### John 20-21 - Resurrection and Restoration

The empty tomb, appearances to the disciples (including Thomas), and a final
scene where Jesus restores Peter after his denial.

## Observations

- The footwashing in John 13 reframes greatness as service - right before
  Jesus' own "hour" of suffering.
- "Remain" (or "abide") is a key word in chapters 14-15 - the relationship
  Jesus describes isn't a one-time decision but an ongoing connection.
- Peter's restoration in John 21 mirrors his three denials - three questions,
  three affirmations, three commissions.

## Questions for Reflection

1. What would it look like for me to "remain" in Christ this week, in
   practical terms?
2. Peter is restored, not just forgiven - he's given something to do. Where
   might that be true for me too?
