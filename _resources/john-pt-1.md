## Overview

John 1-4 introduces who Jesus is and begins showing how people respond to
him - from the prologue's cosmic claims about the Word, down to one-on-one
conversations with Nicodemus and a woman at a well in Samaria.

## Key Passages

### John 1:1-18 - The Word Became Flesh

The prologue sets the tone for the whole book: Jesus isn't just a teacher or
prophet, he's the eternal Word, present at creation, now "tabernacling" among
people.

### John 3:1-21 - Nicodemus and the New Birth

A respected religious leader comes to Jesus at night with questions. Jesus
answers with the language of being "born again" - a complete spiritual
re-start, not a moral touch-up.

### John 4:1-42 - The Woman at the Well

A conversation that crosses social, ethnic, and religious lines, ending with
an entire town reconsidering who Jesus might be.

## Observations

- Each encounter in these chapters moves from a surface-level question to a
  deeper spiritual reality (water, birth, worship).
- Jesus repeatedly meets people where they are - a religious insider, a
  social outsider - and offers the same thing: himself.

## Questions for Reflection

1. Where do I see myself in these conversations - as Nicodemus, the woman at
   the well, or one of the disciples watching?
2. What would it look like to bring my actual questions to Jesus, the way
   these people did?
