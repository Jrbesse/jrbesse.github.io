## Overview

John 5-12 is the heart of Jesus' public ministry in this gospel. The "signs"
(miracles) keep escalating, and so does the pushback from religious leaders,
building toward the decision to kill him.

## Key Passages

### John 6 - Bread of Life

After feeding the 5,000, Jesus reframes the conversation around himself as
the true bread that satisfies - a claim that costs him some of his audience.

### John 9 - Healing of the Blind Man

A man born blind is healed, then interrogated, then put out of the
synagogue - while slowly coming to a clearer and clearer confession of who
Jesus is.

### John 11 - Raising of Lazarus

The most dramatic sign in the book. Jesus weeps with grieving friends, then
calls a dead man out of a tomb - the event that finally pushes the religious
leaders toward action against him.

## Observations

- The bigger the sign, the sharper the division in the crowd's response.
- Physical sight/blindness in John 9 mirrors spiritual sight/blindness - the
  "seeing" religious leaders end up the ones who can't perceive who Jesus is.
- Jesus' emotional response in John 11 (he wept) sits right next to his
  authority over death - both are true at once.

## Questions for Reflection

1. Where have I settled for "bread that perishes" instead of what Jesus
   offers?
2. The healed man's understanding of Jesus grows step by step through his
   interrogation (John 9:11, 17, 33, 38). Where am I in that progression?
